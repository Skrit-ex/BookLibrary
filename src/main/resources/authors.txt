----------------------------|-----------------------|----------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------Leo | Tolstoy | tolstoy.jpg | Leo Nikolayevich Tolstoy (1828–1910) was a great Russian writer and thinker, born in Yasnaya Polyana. He is known as the author of the epic novels War and Peace and Anna Karenina, which became the pinnacles of Russian literature. Leo Tolstoy was also a publicist and philosopher, and the founder of the religious and moral teaching known as Tolstoyanism. Early Life and Education: Born on August 28 (September 9), 1828, he was orphaned at an early age and raised by his aunt. He entered Kazan University but did not graduate. Military Service: In 1851, he left for the Caucasus, where he began writing Childhood and served in the army. He participated in the Crimean War, including the defense of Sevastopol. Literary Work: In 1852, he published the novella Childhood in the magazine Sovremennik, which brought him fame. He later wrote such works as War and Peace (1863–1869) and Anna Karenina (1873–1877), as well as Resurrection (1889–1899). Teaching career: In 1859, he founded a school for peasant children in Yasnaya Polyana, and in 1872 he published Azbuka.
